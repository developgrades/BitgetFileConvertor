Privacy Policy

Last Updated: September 8, 2025

Summary

We do not collect, store, sell, or share personal data. Bitget File Convertor runs entirely in your browser and provides safe tools to convert colors, HEX codes, and Base64 strings into images (and vice versa). All conversions happen locally, and the generated results stay on your device unless you copy them yourself.

This policy is required by the Chrome Web Store because the extension declares a host permission. Below we explain why it exists and how it’s safe.

Data We Collect

None. We do not collect or transmit any of the following:

  • Personally identifiable information
  • Browsing history, keystrokes, or page content
  • Analytics, crash reports, or advertising identifiers

All conversions happen locally in your browser. If you press “Copy”, the content is placed onto your clipboard and never sent to us.

Permissions & Why We Need Them

Permission Why it’s needed What it does not do
host_permissions
https://firestore.googleapis.com/*		 Allows the extension to fetch a small read-only configuration file from our Firebase project. This tells the extension whether to show the built-in tools or load a page from our site. Cannot read user data, browsing history, or third-party content. Only our project is accessed.

We do not execute remote code. The Firebase file is a simple JSON flag/URL — no scripts or analytics are run.

Third-Party Content

Sometimes the extension may open or embed a page from our own site (developgrades.github.io/BitgetFileConvertor). That site may have its own cookies or logs — see its policy if applicable.
